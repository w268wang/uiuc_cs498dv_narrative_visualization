Page 1. World GDP per capita vs Research expenditure

Here, you can see the average GDP per capita and Research Expenditure (ratio of total GDP) in this world from 1990 to 2020.
Here is my assumption: the speed of GDP per capita increasing would be higher (compared to worldwide average) if RE spend is higher.
Data provided by The World Bank (GDP per capita, Research expenditure)

Page 3.

Let' turn our attention to another developed country in North America: Canada.
The assumption somewhat holds :). As that 2002-2008 increased aligned with the worldwide trend when Canada spent around 2% GDP into research.
Also, We can actually see that GDP per capita in Canada stopped increasing starting 2008 when its Research investment ratio dropped to around 1.5%.

Now, let's check the largest developing country today in the world: China.
The assumption still somewhat holds :). As China's GDP per capita growth before 2008 is low compared to the worldwide trend when it spent lower than 2% GDP into research.
But the GDP pp growth rate kept increasing as it spent more in research (especially after 2016).
